Knowledge for Justice

The majority of us were conditioned to think, consume, and conduct our lives in a way that benefits the rich and powerful. To maintain the status quo, they set up systems that keep the masses uninformed to the unjust ways they rule the world. They incarcerate and kill black and brown people at an exorbitant rate; they exploit the environment and underpay workers to cut corners and keep the wealth to themselves; they underfund education to perpetuate cycles of poverty; and they conspire with each other to keep these institutions in place.

But it doesn't have to be this way. We at Knowledge for Justice emphasize self-education as a form of civil responsibility and have therefore compiled lists of resources we find vauluable from thought leaders in various areas. From Ava DuVernay to Ijeoma Oluo and beyond, experts are sharing their knoweldge to inform private citizens of the injustices to which we may be blind.

Additionally, on each page you will find an assortment of charities, nonprofits, and organizations that provide resources to those who need it the most.

Don't see your favorite educational text, informative documentary, or nonprofit listed? Email us at knowledge4justice@gmail.com and we will happily add it!

Thank you for your support and we hope our collective efforts can make the world a better place.

Side Note: In the spirit of reform and revolution, we ask our readers to avoid purchasing from, and thereby supporting, Amazon. Instead, try to find the following resources online, at a local small business, or your public library!
